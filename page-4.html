List of 10 Brightest Stars in The Night Sky

Astronomers express the brightness of stars in terms of stellar magnitude. The smaller the number, the brighter the star. The visual magnitude of Sirius is -1.44, lower – brighter – than any other star. There are brighter stars than Sirius in terms of actual energy and light output, but they are farther away and hence appear dimmer. Normally, the only objects that outshine Sirius in our heavens are the sun, moon, Venus, Jupiter, Mars and Mercury (and usually Sirius outshines Mercury, too).
